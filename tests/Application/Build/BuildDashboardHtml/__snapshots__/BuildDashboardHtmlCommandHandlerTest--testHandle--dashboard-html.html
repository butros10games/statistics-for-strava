TSB

Training Stress Balance (Form) – The difference between fitness (CTL) and fatigue (ATL). Reflects how ready you are to perform.

  • > 25: Risk of detraining - Fitness may be declining due to low recent training load
  • 10 to 25: Peak fresh - Highly recovered and ready to perform
  • 0 to 10: Slightly fresh - Light fatigue with good readiness. A great balance for quality training or longer efforts
  • -10 to 0: Neutral - Moderate fatigue with stable fitness. Well suited for consistent day-to-day training
  • -30 to -10: Accumulated fatigue - Training load is accumulating, recovery days are important
  • < -30: Over-fatigued - High fatigue levels may increase injury or burnout risk. Consider reducing load and prioritizing recovery

Guidelines vary by individual; for more information see Joe Friel's 'The Cyclist's Training Bible' or TrainingPeaks' research documentation.
